# Retained-runset validity audit — 2026-07-12

Scope: every runset under `spikes/nested-x86/results/` retained at PR #98 head
`b6b2a5d`, audited per the re-certification program (bead hm-b5b, item 6)
against the green-on-fail finding: the harness's sole success condition was the
guest `NESTED_X86_L1_DONE` marker, which the L1 init printed **after recording
any gate failure**, so a runset could be recorded green with failed gates
inside. Method: mechanical scan of all 29 retained `console.log`s for
`NESTED_X86_GATE_BEGIN` / `NESTED_X86_GATE_RC rc=N` / `NESTED_X86_GATE_MISSING`
/ `NESTED_X86_L1_DONE` pairing, plus `env.json.rc`, `condition-end.json`, and
appliance-hash provenance cross-checks against the committed build manifests.

Verdicts: **VALID** (evidence stands for what it claims), **INVALID** (must not
support any disposition), **CHARACTERIZATION-ONLY** (real data, but attaches to
a different claim than the disposition used it for), **INCOMPLETE** (run did
not finish; only its own progress lines exist).

## Program-level invalidations (apply across runsets)

1. **Every N-2 hammer runset ran the STOCK backend.** `n2_nested_hammer.rs`
   constructed `KvmBackend::new()` (= `build(false)`), so
   `arm_preempt_exit`/patch-0004 and the patched MTF path were **disabled** and
   `run_until` took the stock SIGIO/debug-step fallback. All N-2
   exactness/overflow evidence therefore characterizes the *stock fallback
   mechanism*, not patches 0004/0005. It is honest data (the landings really
   were exact) but it does not certify the patched work clock. → all
   `n2/cond-*` and the `post-reboot-001` hammer half are
   **CHARACTERIZATION-ONLY**; the N-2 GO is withdrawn.
   (The N-3 repeat gate is unaffected: `boot_patched_payload` builds
   `PatchedKvmBackend`, and N-1's gates likewise ran the patched ABI.)
2. **Appliance provenance gap.** Every retained N-2/N-3 nested runset booted
   appliance `initrd_sha256 981f8b27d6e0…`, which matches **neither** committed
   build manifest (`results/n1/build-manifest.json` = `a6abcc102936…`,
   `results/n5/build-manifest.json` = `6932590dbf8a…`). The tested image was a
   later uncommitted rebuild; its build manifest was never retained. The
   fix set now copies the build manifest into every runset directory
   (`run-appliance.sh`) and hash-verifies artifacts pre-boot; the Phase-2/3
   re-runs will rebuild and re-pin. Until then the 981f8b27 image's contents
   are attested only indirectly (its recorded gate/kernel behavior).
3. **No pre-boot hash verification.** `run-appliance.sh` recorded
   `sha256sum` values into `env.json` but never compared them to a pin before
   boot; recording is not verifying. Fixed in this round; historical runsets
   carry recorded-but-unverified hashes.

  determinism ABI round-trips, L2 boots — **stands** (n1/runset-002, n5).
- Bit-identical repeat evidence at 250–1,000 reps on several conditions with
  one reference hash, nested == metal on three surfaces — **stands but is
  below floors for migration/pause/same-core**, and same-core has no valid
  runset at all.
- N-2 exactness on the *patched* mechanism — **does not exist yet**; the
  Phase-2 re-run (hm-dbh) produces it.
- Perf ratios (N-4) — workload-level ratios stand (they compare gate suites,
  not the hammer); the exact-landing ~5.4×/deadline figure compared
  stock-vs-stock hammers and must be re-measured on the patched mechanism.
